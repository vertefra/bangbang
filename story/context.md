## 1. World Engine: The "Stardew" Philosophy

- **Discovery:** The world is open and discoverable "out of the box." Exploration is rewarded with resources, lore, and secret areas that expand the map rather than gatekeeping it.
- **Foraging:** Skills (Usable) can be found in the wild (e.g., Wild Sarsaparilla for HP, abandoned crates for Bullets).

## 2. The Tri-Path Legacy (Truth Divergence)

The "True Story" of Billy's father and the identity of the Antagonist shifts based on Billy's actions.

### Path A: The Good Outlaw (Silas as Ally)

- **Philosophy:** "Life isn't fair; the law protects the rich, but a moral code protects the people."
- **Father’s Identity:** A "Wild West Robin Hood." Martha kept him a secret to protect Billy from the law’s reach.
- **The Antagonist:** Mr. Sterling (The Bank) and Sheriff Miller.
- **The Twist:** Silas isn't a villain; he's a victim of a system that rewards greed over people.

### Path B: The Lawbringer (Sheriff Deputy)

- **Philosophy:** "Civilization requires order and a shared culture of protection to survive the frontier."
- **Father’s Identity:** A legendary Marshal-Trainer traveling the West to organize police squads. He is a target for every outlaw in the territories.
- **The Antagonist:** Silas "Swift-Finger."
- **Key Ally:** The Edgy Headhunter (Lives in a shack outside town; a grim but loyal friend of Billy’s father).

### Path C: The Renegade (The Lone Wolf)

- **Philosophy:** "The Frontier is dying. Corporations and greedy Outlaws are two sides of the same ruthless coin."
- **Father’s Identity:** A legendary Headhunter. He is a ghost who values freedom above all.
- **The Conflict:** Fighting against the "Corporate Machine" and ruthless bandits alike.
- **The Twist:** Billy’s father doesn't even know Billy exists; he fled to maintain his renegade nature.

## 4. Corporate interests and the Eastern Syndicate

**Corporate interest** in Dustfall is not a single building: it is the alliance of **finance** (the **First National Bank**, **Mr. Sterling**) and **territorial expansion** toward resources (the **Old Mine** and surrounding scrublands).

The **Eastern Syndicate** is part of that same bloc. It is the **claims-and-expansion** face of the operation—survey stakes, “authorized claim” signage, and muscle on the ground (e.g. hired **Bandit Scouts** on **The Old Mine Trail**) enforce Syndicate control of the grade toward the mine. In dialogue and signage, “the Syndicate” refers to this arm; it is **not** a separate faction from Sterling and the Bank, but the **field operation** of aligned corporate interests.

## 5. Initial Character Updates

- **Silas:** A pivot character. Either a misunderstood friend or a primary target.
- **Sheriff Miller:** Either an aging mentor or a corrupt enforcer for the Bank.
- **The Headhunter:** A mysterious figure in a shack; represents the bridge between Law and Renegade paths.
